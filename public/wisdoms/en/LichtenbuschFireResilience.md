# Blessings from Curses

## The Miraculous Story of the Banneux Chapel in Lichtenbusch

In the quiet, wooded area around Lichtenbusch, right on the border between Germany and Belgium, lies a place that at first glance appears to be an ordinary, peaceful Marian chapel. Built in 1968 and dedicated to the "Virgin of the Poor," the Banneux Chapel commemorates the Marian apparitions in Banneux, Belgium, in 1933. But behind this quiet facade lies one of the most unusual and profoundly human origin stories in the Rhineland – a story of transformation, resilience, and the paradox of the sacred emerging from the profane.

### The Paradoxical Foundation: Blessings from Raw Energy

The Banneux Chapel is known colloquially by a far more vulgar name: the "Kiss My Ass Chapel." This name is not a sign of disrespect, but rather a testament to its miraculous origin. To finance the construction and upkeep of the chapel, a local innkeeper ingeniously established a practical "swear jar." Every guest who swore or used a swear word in public at the inn had to pay a small fine into this jar.

From those moments of human anger, frustration, and inadequacy—from curses and raw emotional energy—the financial foundation of a sacred place of peace and blessing was literally created. This transformation is a profound metaphor for human existence: We all carry raw energy within us, but it is up to us to transform it into something lasting and valuable through community and intention. A "curse" became a blessing.

### The Trials of Fire: From Coal to Ash and Resurrection

The chapel's history is also inextricably linked to the element of fire, which is deeply rooted in the Lichtenbusch region. Lichtenbusch was historically known for the charcoal burners' fires and the ore; much fire was needed to extract the ore from the rough rock. This fire was a creative fire of transformation. But the element also turned against the chapel.

It was damaged by fires several times, most severely in August 2022 by a devastating blast of sparks. But the community did not abandon their place of retreat. With combined efforts and donations from both sides of the border, it was rebuilt, a symbol of resilience and a shared identity stronger than any destruction. It was rededicated in October 2023. Just as the old charcoal burners transformed coal into a valuable commodity through heat and pressure, so the community has created a place of renewal from the ashes of fire.

### A Place of Connection

The Banneux Chapel in Lichtenbusch is more than just a building. It is a living testament to the fact that strength can arise from weakness, blessing from frustration, and new hope from destructive fire. It stands as a peaceful monument to transformation and human resilience at a border that does not divide, but unites people.
