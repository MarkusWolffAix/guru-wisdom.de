# Complete List of Living Beings by Element and Spiritual Terms

### Mammals
* **Meerkat** (A well-known mongoose species from Africa)
* **Aardvark** (An insectivorous mammal from Africa)
* **Aardwolf** (An insectivorous relative of the hyena)
* **Ground Squirrel** (A large family of rodents, e.g., marmots, ground squirrels, prairie dogs)
* **Curb Vole** (A vole species native to Europe)

### Birds
* **Ground Woodpecker** (An African woodpecker that nests in burrows)
* **Ground Pigeon** (A genus of pigeons that live primarily on the ground)
* **Earth Thrush** (Various thrush species, e.g., the Siberian Earth Thrush)

### Amphibians & Reptiles
* **Common Toad** (A (of the best-known and most common toads in Europe)
* **Earth viper** (A family of venomous snakes that live underground)
* **Terrestrial turtle** (Family Geoemydidae, often also called Old World pond turtles)
* **Earth lizard** (Genus of squamates in South America)
* **Ground chameleon** (Small stump-tailed chameleons that live in leaf litter on the ground)

### Insects, arachnids & arthropods
* **Bumblebee** (Very common, large bumblebee species)
* **Ground bee** (Often used as a synonym for sand bees, which nest in the ground)
* **Moth moth** (A large family of butterflies/moths)
* **Cutworm** (The caterpillars of moth moths that live in the ground)
* **Flea beetles** (Small, jumping leaf beetles, often garden pests)
* **Ground bug** (A family of true bugs that mostly feed on roots in the soil)
* **Longhorn beetle** (A flightless genus of longhorn beetles)
* **Crane fly** (A large species of midge whose larvae live in the soil)
* **Centipede** (An order of centipedes that hunt in the soil)
* **Ground spider** (Colloquial term for various spiders that live in the soil)
* **Crane cricket** (An old, regional name for the mole cricket)
* **Digger root aphid** (Root aphids that feed on plant roots underground)

### Plants
* **Strawberry** (The well-known red aggregate fruit)

* **Fumitory** (An old medicinal plant with delicate pink flowers)
* **Earth chestnut** (An umbellifer with edible, chestnut-like tubers)
* **Blood clover** (A type of clover that burrows its seed heads into the ground)
* **Terrestrial orchid** (A collective term for orchids that root in the ground)

### Fungi
* **Earth star** (A fungus whose outer layer splits open in a star shape)
* **Earth knight** (A gilled mushroom, e.g., the common earth knight)
* **Earth waxcap** (A fungus from the family Hymenoptera)
* **Earth scalycap** (A smaller fungus that often grows on damp soil)

## 💧 Element: Water

### Mammals & Birds
* **Capybara** (The world's largest rodent)
* **Daubenton's Bat** (A species of bat that hunts close to bodies of water)
* **Water Shrew** (One of the few venomous mammal species in Europe)
* **Waterbuck** (An African antelope species)
* **Water Dog** (Various breeds of dogs, e.g., Spanish or Portuguese Water Dog)
* **Dipper** (A unique songbird that can swim and walk underwater)
* **Water Rail** (A shy marsh bird)

### Amphibians, Reptiles & Fish
* **Water Frog** (A common European frog, often green)
* **Water Snake** (Collective term for many snakes, e.g., the dice snake)
* **Water Turtle** (Collective term for aquatic turtles)

### Insects & Arachnids
* **Water Strider** (Teddy bugs that can move across the surface of water thanks to surface tension) (Water gliding)
* **Water spider** (The only spider in the world that lives permanently underwater)
* **Water flea** (Not a flea, but a tiny crustacean)
* **Dragonfly** (A delicate family of dragonflies)
* **Water scorpion** (A bug with a breathing tube that looks like a scorpion's stinger)

### Plants
* **Watermelon** (The well-known, extremely watery squash)
* **Water lily** (An often poetically used term for water lilies or true lilies by the water)
* **Water hyacinth** (A rapidly growing, free-floating aquatic plant)
* **Duckweed** (Better known as water lettuce)
* **Elodea** (A rapidly growing underwater plant)
