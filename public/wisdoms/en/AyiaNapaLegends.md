# Ayia Napa
## History and Legends of a Holiday Paradise

### 🏖️ From Forest to Holiday Paradise
Ayia Napa in Cyprus is now world-renowned for its stunning beaches (like Nissi Beach) and vibrant nightlife. Originally, however, the region was a dense, uninhabited forest. It wasn't until the mid-18th century that refugees settled there. After the Turkish invasion of Famagusta in 1974, the then-tourism hub, the small fishing village of Ayia Napa rapidly developed into the island's new tourist hotspot.

### 🏰 The Monastery and the Two Legends
The heart and historical origin of the town is the monastery dating back to the Venetian period (around 1500 AD). Its origins are based on two mystical legends:

1. **The Hunter and the Light:** A hunter, guided by his dog, discovered a luminous icon of the Virgin Mary in a cave deep in the forest. This icon was likely hidden there during the Byzantine Iconoclasm in the 8th century to protect it from destruction. The icon became known as *Panagia Napa* (“Holy Virgin of the Woods”) and gave the place its name.

2. **The Venetian Noblewoman:** A wealthy aristocrat from nearby Famagusta fell in love with a commoner. When her father forbade the marriage, she fled to this very cave, dedicated her life to God, and financed the construction of the fortified monastery. She also planted a sycamore tree at the south gate, which still stands in the courtyard today and is over 600 years old.
