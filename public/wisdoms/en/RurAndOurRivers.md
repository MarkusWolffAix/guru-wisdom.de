# Rur and Our
## The Water's Journey from the High Fens to the North Sea

A geographical and creative journey through the river systems of the tri-border region:

**1. The Rur-Meuse System (Westbound)**

* **Rur:** Originates in the High Fens in Belgium and flows into the Netherlands, where it joins the Meuse near Roermond.

* **Wurm:** Originates from several springs in the Aachen city forest and flows north, where it joins the Rur.

* **Urft:** Originates in the Zitterwald forest in the North Eifel region and also flows into the Rur (after passing through the Urft Dam).

* **Göhl:** A tributary that originates near Aachen-Lichtenbusch and flows directly into the Meuse near Maastricht.

**2. The Our-Rhine System (East/South)**

* **Our:** Also originates in the High Fens. We have established that in Germany it was often spelled **"Ur"** in the past, but is now officially called "Our". It forms a long border river and flows into the Sauer near Wallendorf.

* **Sauer:** Originates in the Belgian Ardennes and flows on to Wasserbillig, where it flows into the **Moselle**.

* The **Moselle** originates at the Col de Bussang mountain pass in the municipality of Bussang. It flows to Koblenz and there joins the **Rhine** system.

**Common Basin:** Ultimately, both the Meuse and the Rhine flow into the **North Sea**, where the water from the High Fens rejoins them.

## 3. The High Fens River System
The High Fens is an important European watershed between the Meuse and the Rhine.

### Size Comparison of the Main Rivers
| River | Length | Discharge (Mouth) | Characteristics |

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

**Ourthe** | 165 km | ~ 55 m³/s | Most powerful river, originates in the Ardennes, tributaries of the Venn (55% 31 m³/s). |

**Rur** | 165 km | ~ 21 m³/s | True Venn river, flows into the Meuse (Netherlands). |

**Our** | 96 km | ~ 10 m³/s | Border river, flows into the Rhine (via the Sauer/Moselle). |
