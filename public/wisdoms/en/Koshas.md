# The Five Koshas
## A Journey to Inner Light

The philosophy of the five koshas (*Pancha Kosha*) is an ancient concept from Indian scriptures that helps us understand the multifaceted nature of our being. It originates from the *Taittiriya Upanishad*.

One can imagine the true self—the soul or pure consciousness (Atman)—as an infinitely radiant light. This light is not naked, but surrounded by five layers, similar to the skins of an onion or the nested figures of a matryoshka doll. The word *Kosha* itself means "sheath" or "shell."

These layers range from the gross physical to the most subtle spiritual realm.

### The Journey Through the Sheaths

1. **Annamaya Kosha (The Physical Sheath):** The outermost layer. *Anna* means nourishment. This is our physical body, which comes from the earth, grows through nourishment, and returns to the earth. It grounds us in physical reality.


``` 2. **Pranamaya Kosha (The Energy Sheath):** Deeper down, we find the energy body. Prana is the life force, carried by the breath. This sheath permeates the physical body, enlivens it, and forms the bridge between body and mind.

3. **Manomaya Kosha (The Mental Sheath):** Here resides the everyday mind (Manas). This sheath processes sensory impressions, generates thoughts and emotions. It is the level on which we often spend most of our daily lives, caught in reactions and thought patterns.

4. **Vijnanamaya Kosha (The Wisdom Sheath):** When the mind becomes calmer, we enter the realm of intuition and higher knowledge (Vijnana). Here we find our deeper understanding and moral compass—the place of wise observation beyond mere reaction.

5. **Anandamaya Kosha (The Bliss Sheath):** The finest layer, closest to the inner core. *Ananda* represents pure bliss. This is not a fleeting feeling, but a profound state of being and peace that we touch in moments of perfect presence or love.

The journey of self-realization does not require shedding our outer shells. Body and mind are essential tools. Rather, the goal is to dissolve our exclusive identification with them in order to recognize, through them, the constant, inner light of Atman.
