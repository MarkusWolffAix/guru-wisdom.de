# The Bibliotheca Alexandrina

## A Metaphorical Reflection of Universal Knowledge

### 1. Philosophical and Metaphorical Insights (Conveyed Through the Library)
The library's redesign is a profound, symbolic representation of universal knowledge and human existence. As you learned during a guided tour led by library staff, these are not mere interpretations, but rather the actively lived guiding principles of the architecture:

* **The Incompleteness of Knowledge:** The library's architecturally unfinished circle is the central metaphor for the fact that world knowledge is never complete. There is always room for new discoveries.

* **The Divine and Cosmic Origin:** All knowledge is considered something transcendent, symbolized by the sun, God, or specifically the sun god Ra. The library captures this gigantic, luminous construct.

* **The Earth's Interface:** The planetarium represents our Earth. It stands symbolically at the boundary: On the one hand, it has access to the library's existing knowledge, but on the other hand, it reaches out into the realm of still **unknown knowledge**.

* **The collective responsibility of humanity:** The **Assembly House** represents the gathering of people. Knowledge is not an isolated asset, but must be collectively developed, shared, and preserved. There are also direct access routes to the library from here.

* **Vigilant protection:** Knowledge is precious and must be preserved. This is metaphorically expressed by the library's windows, which are shaped like **eyes with eyelids** and watch protectively over the collected knowledge.

### 2. Architectural and physical insights
From combining your ideas with real-world references (satellite images and the physical model), we adapted the actual features of the modern *Bibliotheca Alexandrina*:

* **The floor plan:** The main building is a massive, obliquely truncated cylinder that appears as an incomplete circle from above. In addition, the spherical planetarium and the angular conference center exist as separate but connected buildings.

* **The Slope:** The striking roof of the main building slopes at a precise angle of **65 degrees**.

* **The Roof Structure:** The grid-like structure of the roof is designed to deflect the glaring sunlight (the "eyelids") while allowing indirect light to enter the vast reading room—the perfect architectural embodiment of "protection."

### 3. Artistic and Musical Insights
We have translated these profound philosophical and architectural concepts into emotional soundscapes and visual artworks:

* **Sublimity and Quest:** The vastness of incomplete knowledge can be musically represented through ethereal ambient sounds, expansive reverberation, and open vowels.

* **Warmth and Connection:** The aspect of the sun god Ra, the protective light, and the gathering of people call for a more loving, warmer musical interpretation with gentle melodies.
