# Evening Affirmations
## For a Peaceful Sleep

These affirmations help you consciously end the day, calm your nervous system, and focus your mind on rest and abundance.

### 1. Focusing on Abundance

"I am grateful for everything I have received today, for what I have recognized, and for what I have not yet perceived."

Expressing gratitude before sleep programs the mind to see abundance. And those who wake up with an awareness of abundance attract different things than those who wake up with an awareness of lack.

### 2. Consciously Letting Go

"I let go of everything I haven't resolved today."

What you don't consciously let go of, your body unconsciously carries during sleep, and you wake up feeling heavier than when you fell asleep.

### 3. Positive Orientation

"Tomorrow I will wake up renewed, with clarity and ready for what is to come."

Your subconscious mind doesn't distinguish between what is real and what is spoken with intensity.

### 4. Emotional Cleansing

"I forgive myself and others for everything that didn't go perfectly today."

Consciously letting go of resentment, frustration, and self-criticism before falling asleep lowers the release of stress hormones and allows the mind to drift into deeper sleep phases without emotional baggage.

### 5. Strengthening Basic Trust

"I trust the flow of life and know that I am safe and secure in this moment."

A consciously cultivated feeling of safety signals to the autonomic nervous system that it can shift from alert mode to the parasympathetic resting state.

### 6. Appreciating Your Body

"My body did its best today, and I now allow it to fully relax and heal."

Consciously appreciating your own body shifts your attention away from constant rumination and toward physical presence, releasing unconscious muscle tension.

### 7. The Mental Closing

"I close this day in peace; everything that was important today has been done."

This prevents the Zeigarnik effect—the brain's tendency to cling to unfinished tasks. It's a clear signal to the mind that its shift is over for today.

### 8. Unconditional Self-Worth

"I am valuable and whole, regardless of how much I accomplished or achieved today."

This decouples self-worth from daily productivity and reduces the pressure of having to think about to-do lists in bed.

### 9. Letting Go of Responsibility

"I now return responsibility for the external world to it and allow myself to simply be."


### This conscious boundary setting protects your mental energy and creates an inviolable space where you don't have to worry about anything but yourself.

### 10. Radical Acceptance

"Everything that happened today is allowed to be as it was. I no longer resist the past."

This ends the mental struggle and the attempt to rewrite the day in your mind. This conserves cognitive energy and speeds up falling asleep.

### 11. Physiological Relaxation

"With each exhale, I sink deeper into stillness and surrender my weight to the surface beneath me."

Focusing on physical release and prolonged exhalation stimulates the vagus nerve, slows the heartbeat, and promotes sleep on a physical level.

**Practical Tip:** Each evening, intuitively choose 1 to 3 affirmations that best reflect what you experienced that day or what you want to let go of.
