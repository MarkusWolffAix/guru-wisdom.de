# Historical Timeline
## Occupiers & Influences of Alexandria

* **331 BC – Founding:** Founded by Alexander the Great and designed by his architect Deinocrates.

* **305–30 BC – Ptolemaic Dynasty:** Alexandria becomes the capital and flourishes as a center of Hellenism. It is the golden age of culture and science (Library of Alexandria, Lighthouse of Pharos).

* **30 BC – 395 AD – Roman Empire:** The city becomes an important Roman province. A time of the "Pax Romana," characterized by a highly multicultural society.

* **395–641 AD – Byzantine Empire:** Alexandria becomes an important center of early Christianity but also experiences intense religious conflicts.

* **30 BC – 395 AD – Roman Empire:** * **641–1250 AD – Islamic Expansion & Caliphate:** After the Arab conquest, the capital was moved to Fustat (near present-day Cairo). This led to a decline in political importance and the gradual Arabization of the city.

* **1250–1517 AD – Mamluk Sultanate:** A rather turbulent period characterized by wars, economic decline, and the fortification and defense of the city.

* **1517–1801 AD – Ottoman Empire:** Alexandria remained an important strategic port but experienced a long period of stagnation overall.

* **1798–1801 AD – French & British Intervention:** Struggle for Egypt between European powers (Napoleon's campaign). This period also marks the beginning of modern Egyptology (discovery of the Rosetta Stone).


* * **1805–Today – Modernity & Independence:** Beginning with Muhammad Ali Pasha, the city underwent a massive reconstruction. It developed into the modern, cosmopolitan metropolis it is today in the Republic of Egypt.
