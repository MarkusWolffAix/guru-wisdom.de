# Mindfulness in Small Things

## Happiness in Big Things

All of life consists of stories; indeed, life itself is one grand narrative. Deep within us, we carry the need and the desire for all these stories—the big ones and the small ones—to have a beautiful beginning and to find a good, peaceful conclusion.

### The Arc of the Day

Each day is like opening a new chapter. It begins with the magic of morning, perhaps with a beautiful sunrise. When we greet this first moment with gratitude, we set the tone for everything that follows. From this moment on, the day unfolds with countless more stories.

Then, as twilight settles over the world and the sun sets, it is time for the grand finale of the day. All the day's experiences are allowed to come to a close. And even if problems or dissonances have arisen during the hours, we strive to resolve them harmoniously in the soft light of evening.


# ### The Poetry in the Everyday

But it's not just the grand dramatic arcs that count. Even the seemingly most insignificant events of the day are their own little stories, worthy of being told with love.

A simple breakfast becomes a tale of enjoyment and care when we experience it consciously. And even something as banal as blowing your nose has its own little drama: You take the tissue, clear your nose, and at the end—as a loving conclusion to this tiny episode—you gently stroke your nose. In this way, even this shortest of stories is brought to a gentle and satisfying close.

### The Great in the Small

One might think that such a perspective is exaggerated. And perhaps it is, in a wonderfully romantic way. But the secret behind it is profound: If we value even the smallest stories of the day and observe them with love, this mindfulness spreads naturally.

The peaceful energy of these small moments accumulates. It carries over into the next hour, eventually filling the entire day with a gentle undertone and ultimately radiating into our whole lives. Those who love these little stories make their entire lives a masterpiece.
